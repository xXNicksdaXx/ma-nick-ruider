This chapter establishes the theoretical foundations of this thesis.
It introduces behavioral programming and feature modeling as the core conceptual pillars.
Based on this, it presents \textit{models@run.time} as the runtime adaptation perspective and explains \gls{glsp} as the technological basis for graphical model tooling.
It concludes with the visual representation theory to ground the design rationale for cognitively effective diagram notations.
